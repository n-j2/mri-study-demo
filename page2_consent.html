RESEARCH INFORMATION SHEET FOR PARTICIPANTS

Study Title: A gamified educational tool to improve understanding of MRI research participation

Based at: University of Nottingham, UK

Faculty of Medicine & Health Sciences Research Ethics Ref: FMHS XXX

Invitation to Participate

You are invited to participate in our research study.

This study is designed to test whether a short computer game can improve understanding of a type of scan called magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and its use in brain research.

The study involves playing a short educational game on a laptop and completing some questionnaires.

Before you decide whether to take part, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please read the following information carefully.

You are welcome to ask any questions.

Participation is entirely voluntary. Even if you decide to take part, you can stop at any time without needing to give a reason.

You are NOT being invited to undergo an MRI scan as part of this study.

Why is this research being done?

This is to test whether a short computer game can help people better understand the basics of:

  • what happens during an MRI head scan
  • how MRI research works
  • how data from research scans are used in scientific studies

The study also explores whether this type of tool may:

  • reduce anxiety about MRI research
  • increase willingness to take part in MRI studies

Who can take part?

To take part in this study, you must:

  • be aged 18–35
  • currently be living in the UK
  • have not previously had an MRI scan

You must also:

  • be attending an in-person session with a member of the research team
  • be able to play a computer game and answer questions (on a researcher’s laptop)
  • be able to understand, read and type in English
  • not previously taken part in this same study

Do I have to take part?

No. Whether you participate or not is entirely up to you.

You may choose not to take part, and you may withdraw at any time without giving a reason.

Even if you decide to take part, you can stop at any time during the study by informing the researcher or simply leaving.

What will happen if I take part?

You will then be invited to complete the three parts of the study on the researcher’s laptop:

Part 1) Pre-game questionnaire (takes most people around 15 minutes)

You will be asked questions about yourself, your views on MRI research, and several multiple-choice questions regarding MRI scans and research facts.

This is designed to take around 15 minutes to complete, but you can take as long as you like.

Part 2) Educational game (takes most people around 20 minutes)

You will be invited to play a short interactive educational game on your computer using your keyboard.

The game is designed to take around 20 minutes to complete, but you can take as long as you like.

Part 3) Post-game questionnaire (takes most people around 20 minutes)

You will be invited to complete a second questionnaire, including similar questions as before, and a short set of questions about your experience of the game.

This is designed to take around 20 minutes, but again you can take as long as you like.

The researcher will be present nearby if you have any questions or concerns during any of these parts.

About the fact questions

The multiple-choice questions about MRI and research facts are designed to be hard. You may not know any of the answers - this is completely expected. There are no consequences for getting answers wrong. If you are not sure, please just take your best guess. You can also leave a question blank if you prefer. Please answer the questions by yourself:

  • please do not look up answers
  • please do not ask others for help

These questions are not a test of you, and they are only used for research purposes.

Only the research team will see your individual responses.

What data will be collected?

During the study, we will collect:

  • basic demographic information you give us (including age, sex, and how often you play video games)
  • your answers to the questionnaires
  • the date, and times, you complete each page, including when you agree to this consent form
  • gameplay data, including time spent playing, game areas you visit, and number of interactions within the game
  • researcher notes of any unexpected events arising during the session

If you provide us with you email address, we will use it to communicate with you about the study if needed. Your email address will not be linked to data gathered about you during your study participation session.

Your data will be identified using a unique participant ID. This allows us to tell that all the data came from a single participant when we analyse the results, without identifying you personally.

Will my data be kept confidential?

Your data will be identified only by a participant ID code and will be kept separate from your email address (if you provided us with it).

Only the research team, and individuals responsible for monitoring, auditing or regulating our research, will have access to data that could identify you.

Your individual responses will be kept confidential unless you reveal something that raises concern about risk of harm to yourself or others. Results will be reported in a way that does not identify you. Anonymous quotes from your responses may be used in publications or presentations (including online), but any information that could identify you will be removed.

After we finish collecting data from all participants, your data will be anonymised and stored securely. For regulatory reasons, this will still include the date and time you agreed to the consent form, but this will never be released publicly. Data will be kept for 7 years after the study has ended and then deleted.

Fully anonymised data from this study (which will not include any information that could identify you) may be shared with other researchers, including those helping to analyse and interpret data. Your anonymised data may be made available in public research repositories to support transparency, reproducibility, and future research. This shared dataset will not contain your email address or any information that could directly identify you.

Can I withdraw my data?

To withdraw your data, you will need to provide your participant ID that will be given to you by the researcher if you decide to take part. Please keep a record of this ID. If you lose your participant ID, it may not be possible to identify and remove your individual data.

You may request that your data are removed from the study up until we stop collecting data from participants and start analysing data. At this point, we will delete your participant ID from the data, and it may no longer be possible to determine which data came from you personally.

To request to withdraw your data, please contact the research team (email listed again at the bottom of this document).

Are there any risks?

This study is considered low risk. However, some participants may feel mild discomfort when thinking about medical procedures. If you feel uncomfortable, you may stop at any time without needing to give a reason.

Will I be reimbursed?

You will not be paid for taking part in this study.

Taking part is entirely your choice.

You may not receive any direct personal benefit, but some people may find the game interesting or helpful in learning more about MRI research.

By taking part, you will also be helping us improve future tools that may support public understanding of MRI research.

What will happen to the results?

The results may be presented at conferences, online or in-print. No one will be able to identify you personally from what we share.

Future MRI research

You will NOT be asked to take part in research involving actual MRI scans performed on you during this study. Your participation in this study will not affect whether you are invited to or can take part in future studies involving real MRI scans.

Who has reviewed the study?

This study has been reviewed by the University of Nottingham Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences Research Ethics Committee.
